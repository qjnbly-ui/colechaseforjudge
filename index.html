About

A prosecutor’s record built in service to this county.

As a prosecutor in the Klamath County District Attorney’s Office, Cole Chase has dedicated his career to serving this county. He is running on public safety, victims’ rights, accountability, and the obligation to uphold constitutional protections.

His message emphasizes courtroom experience, including trial work holding accountable those who steal, those who hurt children, and those who harm the community. That record is reflected in the background listed below.

Qualifications

Built through casework, preparation, and years in the system.

Practice and public service

Cole brings 21 years of legal practice. He is currently the senior Deputy District Attorney for Klamath County, with more than 15 years as a prosecutor. He has previous experience in family law, criminal defense, general tort law, and wills and estate planning.

Teaching and mentoring

Cole served as an adjunct faculty member in the Klamath Community College Criminal Justice program and spent six years as a Youth Peer Court Judge, showing his commitment to educating and serving the County.

Education

Cole earned his degrees from the University of Iowa, including a BA in Political Science and History, an MS in Urban and Regional Planning, and a JD from the College of Law.

FAQ

Short answers to the questions judicial voters actually have.

Can judicial candidates talk about specific issues or future cases?

Not like candidates for legislative office can. The safer lane is to explain background, temperament, qualifications, and commitment to fairness rather than pre-judging disputes.

Why is Cole qualified?

Cole brings two decades of legal experience, service as a Deputy District Attorney, prior youth peer court judging, criminal justice teaching, and public service on the Klamath Falls Planning Commission.

What is this campaign about?

This campaign is about constitutional rights, public safety, victims, accountability, and making sure Klamath County voters choose the next judge.

What is the truth about the gun incident being discussed online?

There’s been a lot of noise on social media about the charges related to the gun incident involving Cole Chase. In this video, he sits down with a member of the community to address it face to face, on camera, with no filters.

Cole’s position is that he pulled a gun in self-defense, was never convicted of a felony, that claims circulating online are false, and that the District Attorney’s Office hired him back because of the kind of prosecutor he is.

The point of sharing the reel here is directness: he answers the questions himself, on the record, and asks voters not to let misinformation decide the election.
